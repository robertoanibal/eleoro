In the world of workflow automation, Zapier is a household name. It’s easy to use and connects thousands of apps. However, for professional services firms—especially law firms—Zapier often hits a wall when it comes to data privacy, complex logic, and long-term costs.

At eleoro, we primarily use **n8n** to orchestrate workflows. Here is why it has become the preferred choice for firms that take automation seriously.

## 1. Data Privacy and Sovereignty

Law firms handle sensitive client data that is subject to strict confidentiality rules. When you use a SaaS automation tool, your data is processed on their servers. 

n8n offers a self-hosted option. This allows us to deploy your automation engine within your own secure cloud environment (such as AWS or Azure). This ensures that your client's data never leaves your controlled infrastructure, making it much easier to satisfy compliance requirements and insurance audits.

## 2. Handling Complex Legal Logic

Legal workflows are rarely "if this, then that." They often involve complex loops, conditional branching, and multi-step approvals. Zapier's linear model can make complex workflows difficult to manage and prone to breaking.

n8n uses a node-based visual interface that allows for intricate, non-linear logic. Whether it's a conflict check that needs to query multiple databases or a document drafting workflow that requires attorney review at three different stages, n8n handles the complexity with ease.

## 3. Cost-Efficiency at Scale

Zapier’s pricing is based on "tasks." As your firm grows and you automate more of your administrative layer, your monthly bill can skyrocket. 

n8n’s pricing model is much more favorable for high-volume automation. Since it is hosted on your infrastructure, you aren't penalized for being efficient. This allows us to build robust reporting and monitoring workflows that would be prohibitively expensive on other platforms.
