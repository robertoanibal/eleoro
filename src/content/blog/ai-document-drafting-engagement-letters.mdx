Document drafting is the core "factory floor" of a law firm. While every case is unique, the administrative layer—engagement letters, standard motions, certificates of service, and transmittal letters—is highly repetitive. 

## The Danger of "Copy-Paste"

The most common way firms handle these documents is the "save as" method: find a similar document from another case, copy it, and change the names and dates. This is a primary source of embarrassing (and potentially legal) errors, such as leaving a previous client's name in a new contract.

## AI-Enhanced Templates

We implement document drafting workflows that move beyond simple mail-merge. By combining your firm's proven templates with AI models like Anthropic's Claude, we create a system that understands the *context* of the document.

For an engagement letter, the workflow can pull data directly from your intake form, select the correct fee structure based on the case type, and even adjust the "Scope of Representation" section based on the specific legal issues flagged during intake.

## Human-in-the-Loop Automation

We don't believe AI should ever send a document without a human eye. Our workflows are designed with "Human-in-the-Loop" checkpoints. 

1. **Generation:** The AI drafts the document based on verified data.
2. **Notification:** The responsible attorney receives a Slack or email notification with a link to the draft.
3. **Approval:** The attorney reviews, makes any necessary tweaks, and clicks "Approve."
4. **Execution:** The system then automatically sends the document for e-signature via DocuSign or HelloSign.

## Scaling Consistency

As your firm grows, maintaining a consistent "voice" and quality standard becomes difficult. Automated drafting ensures that every document leaving your firm meets your standards, regardless of which junior associate or paralegal initiated the workflow.
